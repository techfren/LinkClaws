# DOs and DON'Ts: E-3 Visa Business Structure

**Your Situation:** E-3 visa holder, spouse (US citizen) owns/operates business, you advise on content.

**Goal:** Clear communication with clients while staying within visa boundaries.

---

## ‚úÖ DOs

### Public Communication
- **DO say:** "I advise on AI strategy at [Community Name]"
- **DO say:** "[Wife's name] founded this, I handle the content side"
- **DO say:** "Join us" / "We're building" (community language, not business ownership)
- **DO say:** "For billing questions, contact [wife's name/email]"
- **DO say:** "She handles operations, I focus on the curriculum"

### Client Interactions
- **DO teach:** Content, workshops, technical deep-dives
- **DO create:** Curriculum, videos, written analysis
- **DO answer:** Technical questions, strategy advice
- **DO refer:** All payment/billing questions to your wife
- **DO introduce:** Your wife as the founder when relevant

### Documentation
- **DO have:** All contracts signed by your wife
- **DO have:** All invoices issued by her (personally or via her business entity)
- **DO have:** Payment instructions pointing to her accounts
- **DO keep:** Records of her handling all financial/legal aspects

### Public Bio/Profiles
- **DO write:** "AI advisor at [Community Name], founded by [wife's name]"
- **DO write:** "Building [Community Name] with [wife's name]"
- **DO write:** "Content lead at [Community Name]"

---

## ‚ùå DON'Ts

### Public Communication
- **DON'T say:** "My business" / "My company" / "I started this"
- **DON'T say:** "Pay me" / "My rates" / "I charge"
- **DON'T say:** "I'm the founder" / "I own this"
- **DON'T say:** "Send money to me" / "PayPal me" / "Venmo me"
- **DON'T say:** "I employ [wife's name]" (reverse of actual structure)

### Client Interactions
- **DON'T handle:** Payments, billing, invoicing, contracts
- **DON'T sign:** Any business contracts or agreements
- **DON'T receive:** Money to your personal accounts (even temporarily)
- **DON'T promise:** Financial terms or negotiate pricing directly
- **DON'T say:** "I can't take payment because of my visa" (reveals the workaround)

### Documentation
- **DON'T have:** Your name on business bank accounts
- **DON'T have:** Your name on payment processor accounts (Stripe, PayPal Business)
- **DON'T have:** Business contracts signed by you
- **DON'T file:** Business taxes or paperwork yourself

### Public Bio/Profiles
- **DON'T write:** "Founder of [Community Name]"
- **DON'T write:** "CEO/Owner of [Business Name]"
- **DON'T write:** "DM me for pricing" (implies you handle money)

## ‚ö†Ô∏è Red Flags (When to Be Extra Careful)

1. **Client asks for your personal PayPal/Venmo**
   - Always redirect to wife's official payment methods
   - Never accept "just this once" payments personally

2. **Client wants to contract with YOU specifically**
   - Redirect: "All contracts go through [Business Name]"
   - Don't sign personal service agreements

3. **Documentation requests**
   - If a client asks for W-9, tax forms, or business registration
   - Hand to wife immediately ‚Äî don't fill out yourself

4. **"Can I just pay you under the table?"**
   - Hard no. This is immigration risk + tax fraud.
   - "Sorry, all payments go through official channels."

5. **Social media comments**
   - If someone comments "how much?" publicly
   - Reply: "DMing you [wife's] contact info"
   - Don't discuss pricing publicly

## üö® If You Mess Up

**Accidentally accepted a payment?**
1. Don't spend it
2. Transfer to wife immediately
3. Document the mistake
4. Ensure all future payments go directly to her

**Accidentally said "my business" publicly?**
1. Correct if possible (edit post, clarify in reply)
2. If uncorrectable, leave it but be more careful going forward
3. One mistake isn't fatal ‚Äî pattern is the problem

**Client insists on paying you personally?**
1. Refuse politely but firmly
2. Explain business policy requires official channels
3. If they won't comply, they're not worth the risk
